The Problem

TELUS had deployed 5G infrastructure across Canada but faced a significant adoption gap. Consumers didn't understand the tangible benefits beyond faster speeds, and businesses hadn't been given a compelling reason to upgrade. Awareness wasn't the problem -- relevance was. 5G needed a story that people could feel, not just understand.

The Insight

The best way to drive 5G adoption wasn't to explain the technology -- it was to demonstrate it in contexts people already cared about. Real-world partnerships and physical experiences would create the emotional connection that advertising alone couldn't.

The Solution: ACE

A three-pillar go-to-market strategy -- Accelerate, Collaborate, Educate -- sequencing reach, proof, and understanding in a way that drives sustainable adoption across both consumer and business segments.

1
Accelerate
Targeted ads to engage community segments and boost sales through hyperlocal digital campaigns and incentive programs.
2
Collaborate
Partner with Uber Eats for 5G delivery innovations, showcasing real-world latency advantages in high-visibility contexts.
3
Educate
Pop-up interactive experiences to showcase 5G benefits and drive engagement -- making the invisible visible.
Success Metrics
North Star Metric
Increase in 5G plan upgrades among consumers aged 18-35 within 6 months of campaign launch
  • 5G upgrade conversion rate from Uber Eats partnership campaign
  • Foot traffic and dwell time at pop-up experience activations
  • Aided brand awareness of TELUS 5G among target demographic
  • Net Promoter Score among participants post-activation
Reflection
What I Learned
Marketing strategy is most powerful when it's built around behaviour change, not awareness. The Uber Eats partnership wasn't just a creative idea -- it was a behaviour change mechanism. People would experience 5G latency in a context they already use daily. That's what made it land with the judges and what I'll carry into every strategy problem I tackle.
What I'd Do Differently
I'd stress-test the financial modelling on the pop-up activations earlier. The concept was strong but we could have been more specific about cost per activation and the expected conversion lift to make the ROI case airtight.
Grateful For
Winning this one felt different because it was the most consumer-facing strategy I'd worked on. I'm grateful for a team that pushed the creative work as hard as the analytical work. The best strategies are both rigorous and interesting, and this one was.
