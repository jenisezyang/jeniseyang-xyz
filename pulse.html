The Problem

Every year, critical minutes are lost between the moment a patient is picked up by an ambulance and the moment they receive treatment at the hospital. ER teams are blind until the ambulance arrives: no vitals, no context, no time to prepare. Data collected in the ambulance stays in the ambulance. Paramedics relay information over radio while keeping the patient alive. Handoffs are rushed, incomplete, and missing the details that matter. Every minute of delay increases mortality risk.

Every minute of delay increases mortality risk
+7.5% mortality risk per 30-minute delay
Relative mortality risk 90-min gold standard
The Insight

The gap between ambulance pickup and hospital arrival doesn't have to be wasted. It can instead be turned into preparation time. If hospitals knew what was coming, they could be ready the moment the patient arrived, ensuring a smoother healthcare service. 7 minutes of dead transit time, turned into active preparation time.

The Solution

Pulse connects EMS cameras directly to a hospital ER dashboard. As the ambulance travels, cameras scan patient vitals screens and stream the data in real time. This gives doctors live vitals, risk scores and condition insights before the patient arrives.

📡
Live Vitals Feed
Real-time readings extracted via OCR from ambulance monitor screens, streamed to the ER dashboard as the ambulance moves.
🤖
AI Clinical Briefing
Plain-language patient summary, ranked differential diagnosis and context, generated from structured vitals and ready before arrival.
✅
Preparation Checklist
Auto-generated action items: meds to stage, equipment to ready, specialists to alert, all tailored to the incoming patient's data.

Success Metrics
North Star Metric
Reduction in time-to-treatment for critical patients
  • Decrease in time from when a patient arrives to when treatment starts (door to balloon)
  • Decrease in time it takes doctors to decide what's wrong and what to do
  • Increase in number of times the ER is fully ready before the patient arrives
  • Increase in number of times the AI's suggestions are correct
Risks & Mitigations
RiskLikelihoodImpactMitigation
Poor cellular connectivity or offline areas Medium High Fall back to a direct voice or video call if the state of the patient worsens.
Camera misread or AI generates incorrect diagnosis Low High Multiple specialized AI agents cross validate all scanned values against each other.
Patient data intercepted during transmission Low High All data is encrypted end-to-end. System is designed to comply with healthcare data privacy standards.
Delayed ER dashboard refresh causes physicians to act on outdated vitals Medium Medium Dashboard displays a live timestamp and indicator on the data points.
Reflection
What I Learned
Before Pulse, I thought of product features as things you pick up if they fit, optional additions to something that already exists. Building this PRD changed that completely. Every feature we scoped had a specific problem behind it, a real user frustrated by a real gap. The P0s weren't arbitrary, they were the minimum needed to make the core promise true. That shift in perspective is something I'll carry into every product decision I make going forward.
What I'd Do Differently
Our team came in with very different ideas of what we wanted to build and what winning looked like. That tension was productive in some ways but also meant we spent time questioning each other's choices instead of building on them. I'd invest more upfront in alignment, not consensus, but a shared definition of what success looks like before anyone starts building.
Grateful For
I'm eternally grateful to have worked with some of the most brilliant people I've ever met. Watching how they approached problems and their mindset throughout the entire process was genuinely insightful. I learned more in those 48 hours than I would have had I stuck with the same familiar faces.
