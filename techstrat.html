The Problem

Harbour Pacific Credit Union faced a classic modernization dilemma: their legacy customers relied on familiar systems and in-person service, while younger members expected seamless digital experiences. A full technology overhaul risked losing trust. Doing nothing risked irrelevance. The challenge was to thread that needle -- modernize without alienating.

The Insight

The solution wasn't to choose between legacy and modern -- it was to build a bridge. Credit unions have a trust advantage over big banks. The Triple R Strategy was designed to leverage that trust as a foundation for digital transformation, rather than sacrificing it in the name of efficiency.

The Solution

A three-pillar framework -- Retain, Rebuild, Reach -- that sequences the modernization journey in a way that preserves existing member relationships while building the infrastructure and products needed to compete for the next generation.

R
Retain
Hybrid service models that preserve familiar touchpoints while introducing digital options gradually.
R
Rebuild
Phased core infrastructure modernization -- API-first architecture, cloud migration, data consolidation.

Reach
Mobile-first products and embedded finance partnerships to capture younger demographics.
Key Deliverable: Beacon

The centrepiece recommendation was Beacon -- a multilingual mobile banking app serving Harbour Pacific's diverse member base. Supporting English, French, Spanish, Mandarin, and Punjabi, Beacon enables members to send money internationally, manage portfolios, and access all credit union services in one interface. Full UI mockups were presented with a concrete implementation roadmap.

Success Metrics
North Star Metric
Increase in digital adoption rate among members under 35 within 18 months
  • Reduction in in-branch transaction volume (target: 20% YoY)
  • Increase in mobile banking monthly active users
  • Net Promoter Score maintained above 60 during transition
  • Successful international transfer volume via Beacon in pilot
Reflection
What I Learned
Credit unions aren't just smaller banks -- they're fundamentally different institutions built on community trust. Understanding that distinction changed how we framed every recommendation. The Triple R framework wasn't about catching up to fintech, it was about staying true to what makes credit unions valuable while building the infrastructure to survive.
What I'd Do Differently
I'd spend more time on the financial modelling behind the Beacon rollout. Our qualitative recommendations were strong but we could have been more precise about the cost of implementation versus the projected lift in member retention and acquisition.
Grateful For
Competing against 45 teams and placing 2nd reinforced that clear, opinionated strategy beats comprehensive but vague analysis. I'm grateful to have worked with teammates who pushed back when my thinking was too broad and forced me to get specific.
